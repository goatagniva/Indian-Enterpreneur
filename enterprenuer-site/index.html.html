Top 20 Indian Entrepreneurs (2025)

Ritesh Agarwal – OYO Rooms
Dropped out of college at 17 and joined the Thiel Fellowship. He built OYO from a single budget hotel into a global hospitality brand across 80+ countries.
Deepinder Goyal – Zomato
Started Zomato by scanning restaurant menus at his office. Today, Zomato is a global food delivery and restaurant discovery platform.
Vijay Shekhar Sharma – Paytm
Grew up in a small town and struggled with English in college. He built Paytm into India’s leading digital wallet and fintech platform.
Kunal Shah – CRED
After selling FreeCharge, he launched CRED to reward financially responsible users. His bold ideas challenge traditional banking norms.
Sachin Bansal – Flipkart
Started Flipkart in a small apartment with Binny Bansal. Their vision transformed Indian e-commerce and led to a $16B acquisition by Walmart.
Nithin Kamath – Zerodha
Bootstrapped Zerodha with no external funding. His zero-commission model disrupted India’s brokerage industry and empowered retail investors.
Bhavish Aggarwal – Ola Cabs
Faced rejection from investors but persisted. Ola became India’s largest ride-hailing service, expanding into electric mobility.
Deep Kalra – MakeMyTrip
Left a secure job to digitize travel bookings. MakeMyTrip became India’s leading online travel company, simplifying tourism for millions.
Albinder Dhindsa – Blinkit
Pivoted from grocery delivery to 10-minute commerce. Blinkit redefined urban convenience and last-mile logistics.
Aman Gupta – boAt
Co-founded boAt with a vision to make stylish, affordable audio gear. boAt became India’s top wearable brand through influencer marketing.
Falguni Nayar – Nykaa
Left investment banking at 50 to start Nykaa. She became India’s richest self-made woman, revolutionizing beauty retail.
Harsh Mariwala – Marico
Transformed a family oil business into a global FMCG powerhouse with brands like Parachute and Saffola.
Radhakishan Damani – DMart
A low-profile investor who built DMart into one of India’s most profitable retail chains, admired for its frugality and efficiency.
Ghazal Alagh – Mamaearth
Started Mamaearth to provide toxin-free baby care products. Her brand now leads India’s D2C beauty revolution.
Ravish Naresh – Khatabook
Digitized India’s small businesses with a simple ledger app, empowering millions of shopkeepers and traders.
Rajan Bajaj – Slice
Built Slice to offer credit solutions for young Indians, blending fintech with lifestyle and simplicity.
Anand Mahindra – Mahindra Group
Revitalized Mahindra with global acquisitions and a focus on innovation, sustainability, and social impact.
Narayana Murthy – Infosys
Founded Infosys with ₹10,000 and a dream of ethical leadership. He helped shape India’s global IT reputation.
Shiv Nadar – HCL Technologies
Left his job to start HCL, filling the gap left by IBM. He built one of India’s largest IT firms and invested heavily in education.
